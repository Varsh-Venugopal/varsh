Mediterranean Diet – Focuses on whole foods, healthy fats, and lean proteins. DASH Diet (Dietary Approaches to Stop Hypertension) – A heart-healthy diet rich in fruits, vegetables, and low-fat dairy. Paleo Diet – Based on whole foods that were available to early humans, eliminating processed foods. Keto Diet (Ketogenic Diet) – Low-carb, high-fat diet designed to put the body into ketosis for fat-burning. Atkins Diet – A phased low-carb diet similar to keto but with gradual carb reintroduction. Whole30 Diet – A 30-day elimination diet removing sugar, grains, dairy, and legumes. Low-Carb Diet – Any diet that reduces carbohydrate intake to promote weight loss. Plant-Based Diet – Focuses on whole, unprocessed plant foods while reducing or eliminating animal products. Vegan Diet – Excludes all animal products, including dairy and eggs. Vegetarian Diet – Eliminates meat but includes dairy and eggs. Flexitarian Diet – Primarily plant-based with occasional meat consumption. Zone Diet – Balances macronutrients in a 40% carbs, 30% protein, and 30% fat ratio. South Beach Diet – Low-carb diet focusing on lean proteins and healthy fats. Volumetrics Diet – Focuses on low-calorie, high-volume foods to promote satiety. Raw Food Diet – Consumes only raw, unprocessed foods. Alkaline Diet – Aims to balance the body's pH by reducing acidic foods like meat and processed grains. Nordic Diet – Similar to the Mediterranean diet but based on Nordic foods like fish, berries, and whole grains. HMR Diet (Health Management Resources) – A meal replacement and low-calorie diet. The Scarsdale Diet – A very low-calorie, high-protein, and low-carb diet for rapid weight loss. The Military Diet – A strict low-calorie diet claiming fast weight loss in three days. Medical & Therapeutic Diets Gluten-Free Diet – Eliminates gluten for those with celiac disease or gluten sensitivity. Low-FODMAP Diet – Reduces fermentable carbohydrates to help with IBS (Irritable Bowel Syndrome). Diabetic Diet – Focuses on balanced blood sugar levels through controlled carb intake. Hypertension Diet (DASH Diet) – Reduces sodium and emphasizes potassium-rich foods. Renal Diet – Designed for people with kidney disease, limiting potassium, phosphorus, and sodium. GAPS Diet (Gut and Psychology Syndrome Diet) – Aims to heal the gut with bone broth, fermented foods, and probiotics. AIP Diet (Autoimmune Protocol Diet) – Eliminates inflammatory foods to help manage autoimmune diseases. PKU Diet (Phenylketonuria Diet) – Strictly controls phenylalanine intake for people with PKU. Cultural & Traditional Diets Japanese Diet – Emphasizes fish, rice, vegetables, and fermented foods like miso. French Diet (French Paradox Diet) – Focuses on small portions of whole, fresh foods with occasional indulgence. Ayurvedic Diet – Based on ancient Indian medicine, classifying foods by their effects on body types (doshas). Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) Diet – Balances yin and yang through food selection. Macrobiotic Diet – A plant-based diet rooted in Japanese and Buddhist principles. Mediterranean Diet – Rich in olive oil, fish, vegetables, and whole grains. Nordic Diet – High in fish, root vegetables, and whole grains. Fasting & Time-Restricted Diets Intermittent Fasting (IF) – Cycling between eating and fasting periods (e.g., 16/8 method). OMAD (One Meal a Day Diet) – Eating only one meal per day for fasting benefits. 5:2 Diet – Eating normally for five days and restricting calories for two days a week. Alternate-Day Fasting – Fasting every other day or eating very few calories on fasting days. Warrior Diet – Eating small amounts of raw foods during the day and one large meal at night. Fitness & Performance Diets High-Protein Diet – Prioritizes protein for muscle gain and fat loss. Bodybuilding Diet – Focuses on high-protein intake and strict calorie control. Carb Cycling – Alternates high-carb and low-carb days for energy and fat loss. Vertical Diet – A diet for athletes emphasizing nutrient-dense whole foods. Unusual & Extreme Diets Carnivore Diet – Allows only animal-based foods, eliminating all plant-based foods. Fruitarian Diet – Only fruits, nuts, and seeds are consumed. Breatharian Diet – A controversial and extreme diet that claims people can survive on air and sunlight. Baby Food Diet – Replaces meals with baby food for portion control. Cabbage Soup Diet – A short-term weight loss diet centered around cabbage soup.
